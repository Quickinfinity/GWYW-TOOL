★ Sales Best Practice

Always use physical texture samples the customer can touch. Label each sample with a code. When they choose, note the code on their contract/estimate. This lets them make an educated decision — and puts the responsibility for their choice in writing.

◉ Color Selection
Some customers struggle to visualize finished colors. Physical samples are essential during design consultation to avoid misunderstandings. Printed charts should only be used as a guide — colors shift between printers and inks.
◉
Show physical samples whenever possible to get customer approval on exact color
◉
Explain print variance — printed charts are a guide only, not exact color match
◉
Multi-color mixes vary — staining combinations of 2+ colors produce dozens of unique shades
◉
Document the selection on the contract with sample code or physical sign-off
★ Sales Best Practice

Explain in depth that printed charts are a guide, not a guarantee. When mixing stain colors, show a physical mixed sample if available. Document the customer's approved color code on the contract to protect both parties.

⚠

Protect the Sale — Document the Choice

When texture and color selections are clearly recorded on the contract/estimate with sample codes and customer sign-off, any post-install change requests become paid work — not free rework. A thorough design consultation protects your margin and builds trust. This is not your liability when you've done the sales process right.
